What is FTL Shipping?

Full Truckload (FTL) shipping means your freight gets an entire trailer dedicated exclusively to your shipment. Unlike LTL, your cargo is the only freight on the truck — it moves directly from origin to destination with no stops, no transfers, and no rehandling at terminals.

FTL is the ideal solution when your shipment is large enough to fill a significant portion of a trailer, typically 10,000 lbs or more, or 10+ pallets. Even if your freight doesn't fill the entire trailer, FTL can still be more cost-effective and faster than LTL for shipments approaching these thresholds.

With SwiftLink's FTL service, you get a dedicated driver, a guaranteed pickup window, and point-to-point delivery with real-time GPS tracking from dispatch to final delivery.

When to Choose FTL

  • Shipments weighing 10,000 – 45,000 lbs
  • Freight filling 10 or more standard pallets (48"x40")
  • High-value or fragile goods requiring minimal handling
  • Time-sensitive deliveries that can't afford terminal delays
  • Temperature-controlled, hazmat, or specialty freight
  • Consistent, high-volume shipping lanes requiring dedicated capacity

FTL Trailer Types We Offer

From standard dry vans to specialty flatbeds and temperature-controlled reefers, we match the right equipment to your freight requirements.

Dry Van

The most common trailer type in North America. Fully enclosed with rigid walls, roof, and floor to protect freight from the elements.

  • Interior: 53' L x 8'6" W x 9' H
  • Max payload: 45,000 lbs
  • Capacity: 26 standard pallets
  • ~2,390 cubic feet of space

Ideal for: General merchandise, consumer goods, packaged food, electronics, retail inventory

Flatbed

Open-deck trailer with no sides, roof, or enclosure. Allows top, side, and rear loading with cranes, forklifts, or other heavy equipment.

  • Deck: 48' L x 8'6" W, 60" height
  • Max payload: 48,000 lbs
  • Max freight height: 8'6"
  • Securement: chains, straps, tarps

Ideal for: Steel coils, lumber, machinery, construction materials, oversized equipment

Reefer / Refrigerated

Temperature-controlled trailers equipped with built-in refrigeration units. Maintains precise temperatures from -20°F to 70°F throughout transit.

  • Interior: 53' L x 8'2" W x 8'6" H
  • Max payload: 40,000 – 44,000 lbs
  • Temp range: -20°F to 70°F
  • Capacity: 22–24 pallets

Ideal for: Produce, frozen foods, dairy, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, floral

Step Deck (Drop Deck)

A two-level flatbed with an upper deck and a lower deck, providing additional vertical clearance for taller freight that won't fit on a standard flatbed.

  • Upper: 11' L / Lower: 37' L
  • Max payload: 43,000 – 48,000 lbs
  • Max freight height: 10' (lower deck)
  • No ramp needed for lower deck

Ideal for: Tall machinery, agricultural equipment, industrial components, vehicles

Conestoga

A flatbed trailer with a retractable tarpaulin system that slides open for side or top loading, then closes to provide full weather protection like a dry van.

  • Deck: 48' L x 8'6" W
  • Max payload: 44,000 – 46,000 lbs
  • Full weather protection
  • Side-loading capable

Ideal for: Building materials, metal products, freight requiring weather protection + crane loading

Power Only

We provide the tractor and driver while you supply the trailer. Perfect for shippers with their own trailer pool who need reliable drivers and capacity on demand.

  • Tractor + driver supplied by SwiftLink
  • Your trailer, our horsepower
  • Flexible scheduling
  • Reduced equipment costs

Ideal for: Companies with trailer pools, drop-and-hook operations, seasonal overflow

Benefits of FTL Shipping

Full truckload shipping delivers measurable advantages in speed, security, and cost-efficiency for large-volume shippers.

Faster Transit Times

Direct point-to-point delivery with no terminal stops, no freight consolidation, and no hub transfers. Your shipment moves straight from origin to destination.

Reduced Damage Risk

Your freight is loaded once and unloaded once. With no cross-docking, rehandling, or pallet reorganization, the risk of damage or loss drops significantly.

Cost-Effective at Scale

FTL pricing is based on a flat per-mile rate rather than per-pallet or per-hundredweight. For shipments over 10,000 lbs, FTL often costs less than equivalent LTL rates.

Dedicated Capacity

The entire trailer is reserved exclusively for your freight. No sharing space, no competing for capacity, and no risk of bumped shipments during peak season.

Flexible Scheduling

Choose your pickup date and time. Need an early morning, evening, or weekend pickup? FTL gives you full control over your shipping schedule and delivery appointments.

Real-Time Visibility

GPS tracking on every load with automated check-calls, milestone updates, and ETA notifications. Know exactly where your freight is at every moment.
