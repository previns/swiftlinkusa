What is Expedited Freight?

Expedited freight is a premium shipping service designed for time-sensitive cargo that cannot wait for standard FTL or LTL transit timelines. Unlike conventional freight, expedited shipments move on dedicated vehicles with no consolidation, no terminal transfers, and no delays.

Your shipment is the only shipment on the truck. Team drivers rotate behind the wheel around the clock, meaning your freight moves continuously from pickup to delivery without overnight stops or mandatory rest breaks slowing it down.

Whether it is a critical machine part needed to restart a production line or a pharmaceutical shipment with a narrow delivery window, expedited freight eliminates the variables that cause delays in standard shipping.

Dedicated Vehicle

Your freight is the only cargo on the truck — no sharing, no detours.

Team Drivers

Two drivers alternate driving shifts so the truck never stops moving.

Zero Consolidation

No terminal stops, no cross-docking, no waiting for other freight.

Fastest Transit

Coast-to-coast in as little as 45 hours — the fastest ground option available.

Expedited Service Options

We match the right equipment and driver configuration to your shipment's urgency, size, and sensitivity requirements.

Team Drivers

Two professional drivers alternate behind the wheel, keeping your freight moving 24 hours a day with no mandatory rest stops. Ideal for full truckload shipments that need to cover maximum distance in minimum time.

  • Coast-to-coast in ~45 hours
  • Up to 44,000 lbs capacity
  • Dry van or reefer available

Straight Truck / Box Truck

A versatile option for mid-size expedited shipments. Straight trucks navigate urban areas and tight loading docks with ease, and in some jurisdictions face fewer CDL-related restrictions for faster driver availability.

  • 5,000 - 15,000 lbs capacity
  • Direct dock-to-dock delivery
  • Liftgate available

Sprinter Van

The fastest option for smaller shipments. Sprinter vans are dispatched immediately for same-day and next-day deliveries of 1 to 4 pallets. Perfect for urgent parts, documents, medical supplies, and prototype shipments.

  • 1-4 pallets / up to 3,500 lbs
  • Same-day & next-day delivery
  • Fastest dispatch times

Hot Shot

Hot shot freight uses medium-duty trucks with flatbed or step deck trailers for urgent oversized or oddly shaped loads. When you need heavy or bulky equipment moved fast without waiting for a full tractor-trailer rig.

  • Flatbed & step deck trailers
  • Up to 16,500 lbs capacity
  • Oversized urgent loads

Air Ride Equipped

For freight that demands gentle handling — electronics, medical devices, precision instruments, and fine goods. Air ride suspension systems absorb road shock and vibration, protecting sensitive cargo throughout transit.

  • Vibration-dampening suspension
  • Electronics & medical freight
  • Climate-controlled options

When You Need Expedited Freight

Expedited shipping is the right call when downtime, missed deadlines, or supply chain disruptions cost more than premium freight rates.

Production Line Down

A missing component has halted your manufacturing line. Every hour of downtime costs thousands. Expedited freight gets the part on-site in hours, not days.

Trade Show Deadlines

Your booth materials, displays, and product samples must arrive before the show opens. There is no second chance — expedited ensures on-time delivery to convention centers nationwide.

Seasonal Demand Spikes

Holiday rushes, back-to-school, or unexpected demand surges require rapid inventory replenishment. Keep shelves stocked and customers satisfied with expedited restocking.

Healthcare & Pharmaceutical

Medications, medical devices, and lab specimens often have strict time and temperature requirements. Expedited shipping with temperature-controlled equipment protects patient safety.

Retail Restocks

Empty shelves mean lost revenue. When a fast-selling product runs out, expedited freight from your distribution center to retail locations prevents missed sales opportunities.

Auto Plant JIT Delivery

Automotive manufacturers operate on just-in-time inventory systems with zero tolerance for late deliveries. A single missing part can shut down an entire assembly line costing millions per hour.
